# Known Abuse-to-Femicide Patterns (Case-Derived)

This document synthesizes recurring patterns identified across thousands and thousands of domestic homicide, 
honor killing, and family violence cases.

---

## Pattern 1: Control → Resistance → Punishment

- Woman asserts autonomy
- Male authority perceives loss of control
- Abuse intensifies
- Punitive violence escalates
- Lethal act justified as “necessary”

---

## Pattern 2: Collective Family Enforcement

- Father/brother/uncle involved
- Decisions made collectively
- Female relatives coerced into silence
- Violence framed as moral duty
- Perpetrator feels protected

---

## Pattern 3: Reputation & Honor Trigger

- Rumors, accusations, or perceived shame
- Community surveillance
- Pre-emptive violence to “restore honor”
- High premeditation
- Public justification post-event

---

## Pattern 4: Separation Escalation

- Woman attempts to leave
- Stalking increases
- Threats intensify
- Weapon acquisition
- Lethality within weeks or months

---

## Pattern 5: Sexual Ownership Ideology

- Woman viewed as property
- Sexual autonomy punished
- Violence framed as correction
- High correlation with forced marriage

---

## Pattern 6: Institutional Failure Amplifier

- Reports ignored
- Protection denied
- Abuser emboldened
- Violence escalates unchecked

---

## Pattern 7: “Final Act” Psychology

- Abuser believes control is irreversibly lost
- Violence seen as last resort
- Murder rationalized as justice
