# Historical Continuum: Antiquity to Modernity

This document maps abuse persistence across historical phases.

---

## Ancient & Pre-Modern Societies

Characteristics:
- Women lacked legal personhood
- Violence framed as discipline
- Honor tied to female behavior
- Judicial systems enforced male authority

Correlation:
- Abuse normalized
- Femicide excused or ignored

---

## Early Modern Period (1600s–1900s)

Characteristics:
- Coverture laws
- Women as legal extensions of men
- Domestic violence treated as private

Correlation:
- Abuse hidden rather than eliminated
- Judicial non-intervention reinforced harm

---

## Post-WWII to Late 20th Century

Advances:
- Suffrage
- Employment rights
- Criminalization of abuse

Structural Lag:
- Male-dominated enforcement
- Victim-blaming norms

Correlation:
Legal reform ≠ protection

---

## 21st Century (2000–2026)

Despite global awareness:
- Coercive control outpaces law
- Technology amplifies surveillance
- Institutional response remains reactive

Correlation:
Abuse mutates instead of disappearing.
