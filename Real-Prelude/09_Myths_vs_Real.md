# Myths vs Realities About Abuse and Femicide

This document dismantles common misconceptions that obscure danger and delay intervention.

---

## Myth 1: “It was a crime of passion”
Reality:
- Femicide is typically premeditated
- Warning signs exist long beforehand
- Emotional loss of control is rare

---

## Myth 2: “There were no warning signs”
Reality:
- Signs were present but minimized
- Psychological abuse is often ignored
- Control behaviors precede violence

---

## Myth 3: “He just snapped”
Reality:
- Escalation is gradual
- Violence is rehearsed
- Threats often precede action

---

## Myth 4: “She could have just left”
Reality:
- Leaving increases lethality risk
- Victims face economic, social, and physical barriers
- Control does not end with separation

---

## Myth 5: “It’s a cultural issue”
Reality:
- Abuse exists across all cultures
- Culture is often used as justification, not cause
- Control and entitlement are the core drivers

---

## Myth 6: “He loved her”
Reality:
- Abuse is about ownership, not love
- Control replaces mutuality
- Violence enforces dominance

---

## Core Insight

Myths protect perpetrators and endanger victims.  
Reality-based understanding saves lives.
